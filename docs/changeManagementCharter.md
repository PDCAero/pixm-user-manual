# PIXM Change Management Charter

# Article 1 – Objective

1\.1 The evolution of the Performance Information Exchange Model (PIXM) shall be managed in the interest of the largest number of PIXM stakeholders ("Stakeholders").

1\.2 A Change Control Board ("CCB") with international participation is therefore established, in relation with a Change Control Advisory Board ("CCAB"), both acting under the provision of this Change Management Charter ("Charter").

1\.3 The objective of this Charter is to support the aeronautical information needs of the PIXM user community and of the larger aviation community through a disciplined, inclusive, and transparent approach to developing and approving new PIXM versions.

1\.4 The evolution of the PIXM shall be aligned with the evolution of other ATM information domain models (AIXM, WXXM, FIXM) and with global ATM information reference model developments.

# Article 2 – The Change Control Board ("CCB")

## 2.1 Tasks

2\.1.1 The CCB is the main body that decides on the evolution of the PIXM.

2\.1.2 The CCB:

- a)  schedules the publication of new versions and gives final approval to the set of changes to be included in new versions;
- b)  ensures the transparency of the change management process for all Stakeholders;
- c)  ensures the possibility to contribute in the change management process for all Stakeholders;
- d)  is responsible for the PIXM maintenance and evolution, which includes both the model and the associated documentation (e.g. Temporality Concept, UML to XSD Conversion, Business Rules, etc.);
- e)  may also issue and/or endorse implementation guidelines for new PIXM versions, such as:
  - (i)  a list of new operational (data encoding) capabilities that are supported by a new PIXM version, thus enabling stakeholders to take informed decisions on their need to migrate an existing system to the new version;
  - (ii)  a proposed implementation timeline, thus facilitating the synchronization between the different system upgrades, which is necessary for preserving the interoperability of these systems;
  - (iii)  mapping guidelines between previous versions and the new version, in particular when changes in the model result in a complete restructuring of a concept;
  - (iv)   sample data sets;
  - (v) may also include in its scope the release2 of formal conformance testing specifications, which would enable an implementation to check and claim compliance with an PIXM version;
  - (vi) will establish relations with similar arrangements that might be put in place for other ATM information domain models (MET, Flight, etc.) or for a global ATM Information Reference Model.

- f)   may also include in its scope the release2 of formal conformance testing specifications, which would enable an implementation to check and claim compliance with an PIXM version;

- g)  will establish relations with similar arrangements that might be put in place for other ATM information domain models (MET, Flight, etc.) or for a global ATM Information Reference Model.

2\.1.3   The practical organization and the support of the CCB activities are done by a CCB Secretariat (CCBS).

2\.1.4 The CCB shall be supported by with a Change Control Advisory Board (CCAB).

## 2.2 Decision making

2\.2.1. Decisions by the CCB shall be taken by consensus.

2\.2.2 Therefore:

- a)   The adoption of Change Proposals is a silent process. If no objection is formally stated by a CCB Member, the proposal is considered accepted. The minimal consultation period is three weeks, but this could be longer for important or very large changes.

- b)   In order to prevent that a single CCB Member blocks the adoption of a particular Change Proposal without a solid justification:
  - any objection shall be supported with detailed arguments, in particular on the business impact, so that the other CCB Members have the possibility to understand the reason for the opposition and work for solving the issue;
  - the current operational reality shall be considered as the prevailing argument in the definition and adoption of Change Proposals. Change Proposals that reflect future operational concepts are adopted only when validated by actual operational implementations.

- c)   If consensus cannot be achieved, the disagreement will be documented and the Change Proposal will be suspended until new arguments arise, allowing them to move it ahead or to definitively abandon it. The provisions of the current PIXM version, if any, remain in place.

## 2.3 Membership

2\.3.1. CCB Members shall be PIXM experts with a technical and/or operational aeronautical information background, on a voluntary basis.

2\.3.2. The Change Control Board (CCB) shall be limited to a maximum of 30 members to ensure the efficiency and effectiveness of the change management process. CCB Members shall represent Stakeholder groups rather than individual Stakeholders. To maintain active and balanced participation, the CCB will operate under a rolling board structure, allowing for periodic rotation of members based on participation, expertise, and evolving project needs.

2\.3.3. Applications for CCB membership shall include the following information:

- a) Full name;
- b) Contact details: email and telephone number;
- c) Organization represented and department within that organization;
- d) Stakeholder group represented: Regulatory bodies, Safety Organizations, Part 121 Operators, Part 35 Operators, Educational Interests, Industry/Trade Organizations, ANSP's, Simulator Organizations, Manufacturers, Airports, Drone, etc
- e)  A brief justification of the interest in PIXM of the organization represented, indicating how the current and future PIXM versions are impacting the activities of the organization;
- f)   A brief description of the working experience of the applicant, demonstrating they have a good knowledge of the PIXM and of the aeronautical information domain;
- g)  A statement that the applicant and their organization agree with the Charter.

2\.3.4. All complete CCB membership applications are in principle accepted. However, the existing CCB Members may decide (by consensus) to refuse a particular application, if estimated that it does not meet the requirements stated in Section 2.3.3 above. In such situations, the CCBS will reply to the applicant with a refusal statement, providing a detailed justification of the reasons.

2\.3.5.  CCB Members shall actively participate in the drafting of the Change Proposal and in the effort to achieve consensus.

2\.3.6.   The CCB Members shall follow the following code of conduct when participating in the CCB discussions, on-line or off-line:

- a) Courteous behavior;
- b) Statements made as specific as possible;
- c) Engaging in a constructive manner;
- d) Removal for behavior unbecoming or disruptive determined by CCAB;
- e) Limitation of discussions to PIXM topics.
- f) Working language of PIXM CCB is English. If a message is posted in another language, an English translation shall be provided.
- g) No information violating third party intellectual property rights may be uploaded.

2\.3.7 A CCB Member may be excluded from the CCB in case of serious violation of the Charter or if not following the rules stated in Section 2.3.6 above. The proposal to exclude a CCB Member may be brought forward by any CCB Member and it becomes effective if adopted by consensus by the existing of the CCB members.

## 2.4 CCB Secretariat ("CCBS")

2\.4.1. The CCBS shall provide the technical and human resources for:

- a) The actual drafting of the Change Proposals;
- b) The maintenance of the PIXM files and associated documentation;
- c) The maintenance of the communication means (Website, PIXM Forum, Change Submission and Tracking System, etc.).

2\.4.2. The CCBS shall:

- a) facilitate discussions of the CCB with a view to reach consensual solutions;
- b) mediate in case of disagreements at the CCB;
- c) monitor the ICAO State Letters that announce changes in the ICAO SARPS in order to identify potential changes in the PIXM (with the support of the ICAO AIM Unit);
- d) monitor the evolutions in the ATM domain that might trigger changes to PIXM.
- e) monitor industry standards and common practices that are referenced by the PIXM model

2\.4.3. Currently, IATA and PDCAero-AF provide the CCBS effort, through their CCB Members.

# Article 3 – Change Control Advisory Board ("CCAB")

## 3.1 Tasks

3\.1.1 The CCAB guides and supports the CCB activities (which are at technical/operational level) from a managerial level. It steers the evolution of the PIXM in a way that it can answer the evolving requirements of the Stakeholders.

3\.1.2 The CCAB gives final endorsement for the publication of new PIXM versions and of other CCB deliverables, following an activity report provided by the CCBS.

3\.1.3 The CCAB, based on a technical and operational impact analysis provided by the CCB, may issue a recommendation regarding the implementation schedule of new PIXM versions.

3\.1.4 Authority to interpret.

3\.1.5 Empowered to maintain decorum and discussion is conducted according to 2.3.6

## 3.2 Membership

3\.2.1 CCAB Members shall be members of the management structures of Stakeholder organizations.

3\.2.2 The CCBS is responsible for getting in contact with the Stakeholder groups/organizations listed above and seeking their participation in the CCAB.

3\.2.3 The CCAB Members shall elect a Chairman to act as contact point.

# Article 4 – Meetings

## 4.1 CCB Meetings

4\.1.1 All CCB activities are conducted using on-line tools, in particular a change proposal submission and tracking tool based on GitHub, to which all CCB Members shall have access.

4\.1.2 Occasional CCB Meetings may take place in conjunction with PIXM events, where a significant number of CCB Members are likely to be present. The conclusions of such CCB Meetings shall be recorded in the GitHub, as individual issues/change proposals. No decision shall be taken outside the GitHub.

## 4.2 CCAB Meetings

4\.2.1 The CCAB Meetings shall be supported by videoconference, to enable remote participation.

4\.2.2 The CCAB Meetings shall be prepared and supported by the CCBS, who will also draft the minutes of these CCAB Meetings.

4\.2.3 The minutes of the CCAB Meetings shall be sent no later 3 (three) weeks after the CCAB Meeting to the CCAB Members for approval. The CCAB Members shall approve the minutes no later than 3 (three) weeks after the receipt. Silence of a CCAB Member shall be deemed as approval of the minutes.

# Article 6 – Intellectual Property Rights

6\.1. New versions of the PIXM Model and the associated documentation produced by the CCB shall become and remain intellectual property of IATA and PDCAero-AF.

# Article 7 – Changes to the PIXM Change Management Charter

7\.1. This Charter may be changed at any time by agreement of the CCB Members.
