# PIXM - Home

![](/assets/media/Secondary_PIXM_BlackText.png " =635x342")


Welcome to the home of the Performance Information Exchange Model.  PIXM is developed under a partnership between IATA and the PIXM Development Collaboration and Aeronautical Foundation in an effort to establish a standardized means of communicating Aircraft Performance information to and from the diverse ecosystem of aviation stakeholders.  The ongoing effort to develop and maintain PIXM is thanks to and dependent on the continued collaboration and cooperation of interested and experienced stakeholders like you.


---

## __PIXM Vision and Goals__


 ![](/assets/media/pixmvisiongoals.png " =875x526.5")


PIXM is envisioned as a transformative initiative for the aviation industry, ultimately serving as a broad and useful standard that enables efficient and understandable exchange of performance data among various stakeholders. PIXM is intended to become the primary mechanism by which performance questions and answers can be translated seamlessly between airlines, manufacturers, regulatory bodies, and other aviation parties. By creating a common language for performance information, PIXM has the potential to significantly enhance operational safety, efficiency, and collaboration across the industry.

* **__Expand aircraft performance and flight operations engineering calculation capabilities to cover all industry needs__:** 
  * PIXM's information exchange model is designed to encompass a broad set of performance parameters derived from diverse performance engineering and flight operations contexts. By using extensible schemas and descriptive structures, it can represent both legacy SCAP data and modern flight operations and calculation logic, allowing integration and collaboration with emerging industry tools, OEM-specific models, regulatory frameworks.

* **__Increase insight and collaboration between aviation stakeholders__**
  * Through a shared and semantically rich data model, the PIXM approach enables operators, OEMs, regulators, and software providers to interpret and exchange performance data with minimal ambiguity. The use of linked identifiers, standardized metadata, and schema alignment (e.g. with AIRM or GML) leads to traceability and transparency across domains, encouraging better cross-stakeholder collaboration and innovation.

* **__Minimize the occurrence of redundant, inaccurate or inappropriate calculations__**
  * The structured nature of PIXM's schema—with explicit units, constraints, and traceable sources—helps enforce consistent business rules and validation logic. This ensures that performance calculations are derived from the same authoritative datasets and interpretations across applications.

* **__Increase accountability and repeatability of aircraft performance calculations__**
  * Each PIXM data exchange instance can carry version-controlled metadata, source annotations, and configuration flags, ensuring that calculations can be repeated or audited. This supports regulatory oversight, internal QA, and continuous improvement processes in flight operations engineering.

* **__Enhance IP Protection for the OEM and Operators__**
  * The PIXM model provides a mechanism for separating proprietary logic and data (e.g.  proprietary performance results or the calculators themselves) from the data exchanged across systems. PIXM will allow for secure encapsulation of IP while still enabling operators and vendors to work with standardized data interfaces and workflows.

* **__Reduce the growing risk of distributed models__**
  * Accurate aircraft performance data is valuable, and its value is becoming recognized in more corners of the industry. By establishing a central, well-documented, and extensible schema, PIXM reduces the need for each stakeholder to create and maintain their own unique performance models. This limits fragmentation and reduces the risk for propagation of inaccurate data, misinterpretation of accurate data, or unauthorized use of proprietary data as models are distributed across users and platforms.

* **__Encourage educational and professional development in aircraft performance and flight operations engineering__**
  * The standardization and clarity provided by the PIXM data model make it easier to teach performance engineering concepts in an accessible, modern format. With open documentation, sample datasets, and model-driven APIs, the next generation of engineers can more quickly gain fluency in industry practices and contribute to their advancement.

### Preliminary Development Plan

 ![](/assets/media/image.png " =1001x506")


PIXM's initial development focus on the adaptation of the existing IATA SCAP Interface specifications for both Takeoff (v25) and Landing (v22) to an XML specification which aligns with the ATM Information Reference Model standards.  The SCAP standards represent the current most thorough collection of computerized aircraft performance parameters and the most complete interface specification for the state-of-the-art aircraft performance calculators in use today.  The initial development of PIXM has also taken into account and adopted data types, structures and definitions from the other AIRM-compliant exchanges already in existence to ensure immediate interoperability between the existing exchange models and PIXM.
